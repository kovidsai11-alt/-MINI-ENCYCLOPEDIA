Science & Nature

Overview • Key concepts • How to learn

The scientific method: observe, hypothesize, experiment, analyze, and revise. Science builds models that explain natural phenomena with testable predictions. Major branches include physics (study of matter, energy, forces), chemistry (substances and reactions), earth sciences (planetary processes), and astronomy (cosmos).

Physics — fundamentals
Classical mechanics describes motion and forces (Newton's laws). Thermodynamics covers heat, energy, and entropy. Electromagnetism unifies electric and magnetic fields; Maxwell's equations are central. Modern physics includes special and general relativity (space, time, gravity) and quantum mechanics (behaviour at atomic scales).
Chemistry — essentials
Atoms, elements, periodic trends, chemical bonding (ionic, covalent, metallic), and reaction energetics. Organic chemistry studies carbon compounds; biochemistry focuses on molecules of life (proteins, nucleic acids, lipids, carbohydrates).
How to learn science effectively
Use active learning: solve problems, perform small experiments, explain concepts aloud, and connect math to physical intuition. Prioritise conceptual clarity before memorization. Read papers and replicate simple experiments when possible.

Technology & Computing

Hardware • Software • Internet • AI

Computing spans algorithms, data structures, computer architecture, networking, and software engineering. Modern technology trends include cloud computing, distributed systems, mobile apps, cybersecurity, and machine learning/AI.

Software Development

Key practices: version control (git), automated testing, continuous integration/deployment (CI/CD), modular design, and documentation. Languages vary by task: Python for scripting/ML, JavaScript for web frontends, Java/C# for enterprise, C/C++ for systems.

Machine Learning & AI

ML uses data to learn patterns. Supervised learning maps inputs to labels; unsupervised finds structure in data; reinforcement learning learns via rewards. Neural networks power recent advances. Important topics: model evaluation, overfitting, regularization, and explainability.

Cybersecurity — basic hygiene
Use strong unique passwords, two-factor authentication, keep software updated, use reputable antivirus/firewalls, understand phishing tactics, and backup important data. For development: follow secure coding practices and review dependencies.

Mathematics

Arithmetic to advanced topics

Mathematics is language of patterns, structures, and proofs. Major areas: algebra, calculus (limits, derivatives, integrals), linear algebra (vectors, matrices), probability & statistics, discrete math, and numerical methods. Math underpins physics, engineering, CS, finance, and data science.

How to study math
Practice deliberately: solve diverse problems, write proofs, explain solutions line-by-line, and revisit mistakes. Use visual intuition where possible (graphs, diagrams). Build foundational skills before tackling advanced topics.

World History

Broad sweep • Civilizations • Turning points

History traces human societies — migrations, state formation, trade, revolutions, technology, and culture. Key turning points: agricultural revolution, rise of cities/empires, scientific revolution, industrial revolution, world wars, decolonization, and the digital revolution.

How to read history critically
Evaluate primary sources, consider multiple perspectives, watch for bias, and connect events across time and place. Understand economic, social, political, and cultural causes for historical change.

Geography & Environment

Physical geography • Climate • Human-environment interaction

Geography covers Earth's surface, climate systems, ecosystems, landforms, and human impacts. Climate change, biodiversity loss, urbanization, and sustainable resource management are central challenges.

Climate change — essentials
Greenhouse gases trap heat, global average temperatures are rising, resulting in sea-level rise, changing weather patterns, and ecological disruption. Mitigation (reduce emissions) and adaptation (prepare societies) are both necessary.

Biology & Medicine

Cells, evolution, health

Biology studies life from molecules up to ecosystems. Genetics explains inheritance; evolution by natural selection explains diversity. Human health merges biology with clinical practice; public health focuses on populations, prevention, and health systems.

Genetics & evolution
DNA encodes genes; mutations create variation; natural selection and genetic drift shift allele frequencies over generations. Modern genomics uses high‑throughput sequencing to study genomes at scale.

Arts & Literature

Creativity • Criticism • History

Art and literature express human experience across media: painting, music, theater, film, and text. Literary techniques include metaphor, narrative voice, imagery, and structure. Study works in historical context and analyze themes, form, and cultural impact.

How to read literature closely
Annotate texts, note recurring motifs, examine diction and syntax, and connect form to meaning. Compare interpretations and consult scholarly commentary for depth.
